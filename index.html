Litigation & Recent News

Web accesibilty and case law has a storied history but the most relevent precedent for our clients was established in 2019.
In late 2019 the supreme court refused to hear a petition from Domino's Pizza to dispute a lower court ruling that the pizza chains website is required to be accesible to the disabled. This left the lower court's decision as precedent.

What does this mean for you?
This ruling means that an entity conducting business online must make their websites acccesible to the disabled. Specifically in comliance with WCAG, 508, and the ADA. This is a giant win for accesibility advocates but also a potential pitfall to the unaware businesses.
Some resistance to the ruling has been voiced but it doesnt stand to the simple analogy of a building being required to have a wheelchair accesible ramp. Being and ally to the visually impaired community means going above and beyond to ensure your digital footprint is not only accesible but not providing a second rate experience for disabled consumers.
